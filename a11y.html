Accessibility audit

My Role

  • UX Designer with a focus on Accessibility. I leveraged my knowledge of WCAG guidelines and assistive technologies to ensure our digital platforms are usable by a wider audience, fostering a more inclusive user experience.
  • Project manager. Due to limited team capacity, I had to perform some of the tasks of a project manager, especially the ones that concern the initial stages of a project.

What I did

  • Project definition, justification, and planning
  • Stakeholder identification and management
  • Automated and manual website testing and findings presentation
  • Mid-Fi Wireframes
  • Hi-Fi mock-ups (together with UI designer)
  • Components for Design System (together with UI designer)
  • Accessibility statement prepparation

Challenge

In the design team, we realized that our digital platforms were not accessible for people having different ways of using the web, especially for those using assistive technology. As website redesign was already in the backlog, we knew that it was the time to plan with accessibility as a success criterion for design and development.

On top of the moral motivation to make our platforms accessible, there was a legal one. Our business was preparing for the expansion to other markets where Accessible legislation was already in effect, and European Accessibility Act would come into effect across the EU in the near future.

Result

This project resulted in a comprehensive website audit with the list of uncomformities with the guidelines, links to the guidelines and their success criteria. This audit was later used to add tasks to the design and development backlogs to fix the issues.
