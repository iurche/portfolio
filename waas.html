Wheels as a service

The goal of this project was to enable Lynk & Co users to enjoy a smooth process of ordering the company’s latest service: Wheels service.

Within this project, we have designed and implemented the flow for users with the entry points, business logic, limitations, error states and edge cases.

Challenge

There were going to be different experiences for users depending on such conditions. Therefore, the PO, the BA, and the design team were facing two challenges.

On top of the moral motivation to make our platforms accessible, there was a legal one. Our business was preparing for the expansion to other markets where Accessible legislation was already in effect, and European Accessibility Act would come into effect across the EU in the near future.

The flow design

We had to design an effective, simple and converting user flow, that would effectively answer user questions about the service and prompt users to purchase the service.

Error states and edge cases

We had to work closer with the sales team, logistics and the back office to identify the cases users might face, how to minimize their impact and how to avoid user frustration.

Lean design process

This project had strict deadlines since the wheel shift happened only during certain seasons for a relatively short period. Since the revenue from this project was projected to be significantly high, the decision was made to follow the Lean design methodology.

These are the steps we took to complete this project:

  1. Gathering requirements from the stakeholders.
  2. Mapping the flow with the BAs.
  3. Mapping the edge cases and error messages with the tech team.
  4. Proposing the high fidelity design using the design system.
  5. Gathering feedback, reiterating the design and presenting the proposal again (3x times).
  6. After getting a final approval from the stakeholders, signing the design off with the development team.
  7. Desk checks and testing interface in the User Acceptance Testing environment.
  8. Gathering performance and adding the improvement task to the upcoming design sprints.

Impact

Product page for different users

We have designed the Product description page for eligible users and ineligible/logged-out ones. This was needed since the service itself as well as its price was different depending on the market. So we decided to display the price only to eligible logged-in users.

Entry points

In the flow, we have suggested three entry points:

  • Emails.
  • Subscription page (since the offer was available only to subscription users).
  • User profile in the newly added section “Products & services.”
